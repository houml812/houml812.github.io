Berlin Poster

After studying abroad in Berlin during the summer of 2019, I was inspired by the style of graphic design that I saw all across the city. In comparison to other European capitals, Berlin felt more chic and modern with straightforward blocks, sharp edges, and monotone color schemes. In particular, I wanted to emulate the brutalist-ic style that I not only saw in the design work and advertising in Berlin, but also the rising global trend of thick blocked letters and bold, outlined text.

In the creation of this poster, I tried to play with the organization of information and the spacing of the blocks. I kept it relatively simple, using only one font throughout the piece. So, in order to make it more visually interesting but still cohesive, I tried many different iterations of positioning the black block in the background as well as the two white rectangular frams and the picture of the Brandenburg Gate. I also experimented with having the locations that I visited be positioned vertically along the top right side, which I thought made the page look a bit more interesting and filled up the whitespace nicely.
